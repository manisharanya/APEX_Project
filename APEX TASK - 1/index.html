Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants prepare their own food using sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide.

This process mainly occurs in the leaves of plants inside chloroplasts. Chlorophyll present in leaves absorbs sunlight and converts light energy into chemical energy.

During photosynthesis, plants take carbon dioxide from the atmosphere and water from the soil to produce glucose and oxygen.

Chemical Equation

Carbon Dioxide + Water + Sunlight → Glucose + Oxygen

Interesting Facts

  • Photosynthesis occurs mainly in green plants.
  • Plants release oxygen during this process.
  • It helps maintain oxygen balance in atmosphere.
  • Plants are called producers because they make their own food.

Importance of Photosynthesis

Photosynthesis is essential for life on Earth because it produces oxygen required for respiration by humans and animals.

It also removes carbon dioxide from atmosphere and helps maintain ecological balance. Without photosynthesis, there would be no food chain and no oxygen supply.

Plants provide food directly or indirectly to all living organisms. Therefore, photosynthesis is considered one of the most important biological processes.

Benefits of Photosynthesis

  • Produces oxygen
  • Maintains food chain
  • Supports life on Earth
  • Reduces carbon dioxide levels
  • Provides food for living organisms
